Medellín does not yet have a **Michelin‑inspected guide**, so no restaurant here officially carries a Michelin star. However, several places offer **Michelin‑level tasting‑menu experiences** in quality, technique, and presentation. Below are the top spots known for ambitious, multi‑course tasting menus worth treating as “Michelin‑class.”

---

## 1. **Elcielo Medellín** – El Poblado

- **Concept**: Sensory, multi‑course tasting menu blending Colombian ingredients with avant‑garde techniques (inspired by the chef’s **Michelin‑starred El Cielo in Miami/Washington, D.C.**).elcielo+2
    
- **Tasting‑menu style**:
    
    - “The Experience” tasting menu (around **12–18 courses**) including interactive elements like “Chocolate Therapy.”havenmedellin+1
        
    - Optional wine pairings (4‑ or 6‑glass options).[elcielo](https://elcielo.com.co/medellin/the-experience/)
        
- **Price range**: Roughly **COP 600k–700k per person** without pairing; add **COP 300k** for 4‑glass wine pairing.courtneymuro+1
    

**Why it counts**: It is repeatedly described as **Michelin‑star‑level** by travel and food writers and is housed inside the upscale Elcielo Hotel in El Poblado.elcielohotel+2

---

## 2. **Carmen Medellín** – El Poblado

- **Concept**: Modern, contemporary Colombian tasting menu with a focus on **local ingredients and refined plating**.carmenmedellin+1
    
- **Tasting‑menu style**:
    
    - Multi‑course format (often 5–7 courses) with optional wine pairings.
        
    - Signature dishes include shrimp ceviche with mango and coconut, and “posta negra” (meat cooked in panela) with plantain purée.[havenmedellin](https://havenmedellin.com/five-star-gastronomic-experiences-in-medellin-a-complete-guide-for-food-lovers/)
        
- **Price range**: Around **COP 500k–700k per person** depending on pairing choices.courtneymuro+1
    

**Why it counts**: Frequently called one of the **two most Michelin‑worthy restaurants in Medellín** (alongside Elcielo) by food‑travel guides.havenmedellin+1

---

## 3. **Ocio / Oci.mde** – El Poblado

- **Concept**: Contemporary Latin–fusion tasting menu emphasizing **textural contrasts and seasonal ingredients**.wanderlog+1
    
- **Tasting‑menu style**:
    
    - Multi‑course “experience” menu with optional wine or cocktail pairings.
        
    - Dishes rotate but often feature seafood, slow‑cooked meats, and creative vegetable courses.tripadvisor+1
        
- **Price range**: Typically **COP 450k–650k per person** for the full tasting.[wanderlog](https://wanderlog.com/list/geoCategory/61255/michelin-star-restaurants-in-medellin)
    

**Why it counts**: Listed among Medellín’s top “Michelin‑style” fine‑dining spots in curated city‑gastronomy guides.tripadvisor+1

---

## 4. **La Sere by Juanma** – El Poblado

- **Concept**: Upscale Italian tasting‑style menu inside the Elcielo ecosystem, by chef Juanma Barrientos.[elcielohotel](https://www.elcielohotel.com/enblog/top-restaurants-medellin-fine-dining)
    
- **Tasting‑menu style**:
    
    - Multi‑course Italian–fusion menu (pasta, risotto, and luxury proteins) with wine pairings.
        
    - Emphasis on handmade pasta and premium ingredients.[elcielohotel](https://www.elcielohotel.com/enblog/top-restaurants-medellin-fine-dining)
        
- **Price range**: Roughly **COP 400k–600k per person** including pairing.[elcielohotel](https://www.elcielohotel.com/enblog/top-restaurants-medellin-fine-dining)

## 5. **Alambique** – El Poblado

- **Concept**: Contemporary Colombian tasting menu with a strong bar and cocktail program.wanderlog+1
    
- **Tasting‑menu style**:
    
    - Multi‑course “experience” menu with optional cocktail or wine pairings.
        
    - Known for bold flavors and modern reinterpretations of regional dishes.[wanderlog](https://wanderlog.com/list/geoCategory/61255/michelin-star-restaurants-in-medellin)
        
- **Price range**: Around **COP 400k–550k per person**.[wanderlog](https://wanderlog.com/list/geoCategory/61255/michelin-star-restaurants-in-medellin)
