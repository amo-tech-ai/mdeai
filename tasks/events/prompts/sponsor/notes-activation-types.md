## 1. Full list of activation types (core + advanced + innovative)

## A. Social media activations

- Instagram static / carousel ads + Stories / Reels spots for sponsors.
    
- TikTok branded challenges (“dance + sponsor tag”).
    
- WhatsApp‑based campaigns: broadcast lists, status‑based polls, “tell‑a‑friend” referral flows.
    
- YouTube pre‑roll and mid‑roll sponsor integrations.
    
- Influencer co‑creation: unboxings, behind‑the‑scenes, “day‑in‑the‑life” tied to events.
    
- User‑generated content (UGC) campaigns with hashtags tied to mdeai events.
    
- Giveaways and voting campaigns (e.g., “vote your favorite act → sponsor giveaway”).
    

## B. Website / digital activations

- Persistent header / leaderboard banners.
    
- Sidebar / sidebar sticky banners.
    
- “Sponsored” listings in search results and event‑detail pages.
    
- Embedded sponsor cards inside chat‑bot or AI assistant responses.
    
- Referral links with UTM‑tagged sub‑codes per sponsor.
    
- Sponsored landing pages for high‑value sponsors (e.g., “VIP Lounge powered by X”).
    

## C. Event / on‑site activations

- Title sponsorship (e.g., “mdeai x [Brand] Experience”).
    
- Stage naming and branding on AV screens.
    
- LED wall wraps, floor decals, and directional signage.
    
- QR‑code activations: scan to vote, scan to win, scan to unlock VIP info.
    
- Audio‑visual sponsor integrations (e.g., “this set brought to you by…” on‑screen overlays).
    

## D. Experiential activations

- Interactive booths with photo‑ops, emojis, and instant social sharing.
    
- AR filters / QR‑code AR experiences (e.g., point your phone at venue artwork to unlock a mini‑game).
    
- VR mini‑experiences (e.g., “VIP backstage pass” 360° tour).
    
- Gamification: spin‑to‑win, spin wheels, challenge ladders, “earn badges” for engagement.
    
- Live competitions and voting (e.g., “Fan’s Choice Award” live during the show).
    

## Advanced activations

- Real‑time leaderboards: “Top 3 sponsors by engagement this hour.”
    
- Second‑screen experience: mobile app that syncs with event AV (live polls, sponsor‑branded overlays).
    
- Personalized sponsor content: recommendation engine that shows different sponsor banners / offers based on user behavior and profile.
    
- Geo‑targeted activations: when users are near event venues, push WhatsApp / SMS / app notifications with sponsor offers.
    
- Influencer amplification tied to events: pre‑event, during‑event, and post‑event sponsored content stacks.
    
- Hybrid online‑offline campaigns: door‑access codes tied to sponsor‑driven social shares or referrals.
    

## Innovative / cutting‑edge ideas

- Dynamic pricing of sponsorship slots: AI increases / decreases price per slot based on event popularity, sponsor demand, and remaining inventory.
    
- AI‑generated sponsor creatives: brand‑safe, on‑brand banners, Reels cuts, and carousel assets auto‑generated per sponsor.
    
- Performance‑based pricing: CPL (cost‑per‑lead), CPA (cost‑per‑action) for SMS sign‑ups, QR scans, or app downloads.
    
- Live ROI dashboards visible during event: sponsors see real‑time KPIs on their own mini‑screen or iPad.
    
- NFT / digital collectibles for sponsors: limited‑edition mdeai “moment” tokens tied to specific events or performances.
    
- AI‑generated highlight reels: automatically stitched 15–30s clips of each sponsor’s visibility, sent post‑event as a performance report.
    
- Gamified referral systems: attendees earn points for bringing friends; points redeemable for sponsor‑branded perks.

## 3. AI features and agent workflows

## A. AI content generation

- Prompt‑engine that ingests sponsor brand guidelines and outputs:
    
    - Instagram captions, Reels concepts, TikTok hooks.
        
    - WhatsApp‑friendly CTAs and poll copy.
        
    - Banner copy variations for each placement (header, sidebar, chat‑card).
        
- Auto‑generation of sponsor‑specific creatives per channel (e.g., “Instagram‑square” vs “Story” vs “TikTok vertical”).
    
- Batch‑generation of 3–5 ad‑variant sets per sponsor per campaign.
    

Workflow:

1. Sponsor uploads brand deck / key messages.
    
2. AI indexes brand voice, colors, and CTAs.
    
3. System auto‑generates creatives for each activation slot.
    
4. Human curator approves or tweaks; AI learns from edits.
    

## B. AI matchmaking

- Inputs: event type, audience demographics, past engagement data, sponsor vertical.
    
- Matching engine:
    
    - “Sponsor ↔ event” fit score (e.g., beauty brand + Colombiamoda‑style events).
        
    - “Sponsor ↔ audience” fit score (e.g., nightlife‑focused sponsors match with party‑oriented events).
        
    - Recommended activation types based on historical performance.
        

Agents:

- `matchmaker_agent` watches new events and new sponsors, then flags high‑potential matches.
    
- `package_recommender_agent` suggests package tiers (Bronze / Silver / Gold) with projected ROI.
    

## C. AI insights

- Multimodal analytics engine that:
    
    - Tracks scans, clicks, shares, and app sessions per sponsor.
        
    - Compares performance vs baseline and vs competitors.
        
    - Outputs natural‑language explanations, e.g.:
        
        - “This activation is performing 32% better because your QR was placed near the bar entrance instead of the restroom.”
            
        - “Your TikTok challenge had 41% higher completion because the CTA was under 10 seconds.”
            

## D. AI optimization

- Recommends:
    
    - Move placement (e.g., “move banner from sidebar to header”).
        
    - Change creative (e.g., “try a version with a human face and event‑specific text”).
        
    - Adjust timing (e.g., “push WhatsApp broadcast at 8 PM vs 6 PM”).
        
- Uses A/B test data and attribution models (first‑click, last‑click, data‑driven) to prioritize changes.
    

## E. AI agents (OpenClaw‑style workflows)

1. **Sponsor discovery agent**
    
    - Scrapes local business directories, event‑related Google Ads, Instagram hashtags (#MedellínNoche, #Colombiamoda2025), and LinkedIn.
        
    - Builds a prospect list of brands already running events, nightlife, or influencer‑campaigns in Medellín.
        
2. **Competitor‑analysis agent**
    
    - Monitors sponsor‑brands running similar campaigns elsewhere.
        
    - Outputs “competitor benchmark” scores into the dashboard.
        
3. **Outreach agent**
    
    - Auto‑generates personalized emails and WhatsApp messages.
        
    - Uses enrichment (Clay / Apollo) to find contact info.
        
    - Sends follow‑ups and escalates hot leads into a CRM.
        
4. **Performance‑monitoring agent**
    
    - Runs every 15–30 minutes:
        
        - Checks KPIs per sponsor.
            
        - Triggers alerts or auto‑optimizations (e.g., pause low‑performing creatives).
            
    - Sends sponsors a daily “snapshot” via WhatsApp or email.
        
5. **Workflow‑orchestration agent**
    
    - Coordinates:
        
        - Discovery → enrichment → outreach → negotiation → contract upload → activation kickoff → reporting.
            
    - Ensures no prospect falls into a black hole.

## 4. Tool stack recommendation

## Discovery

- **Best**: OpenClaw + Firecrawl / PhantomBuster + Google Trends / LinkedIn‑based scraping.
    
- Why: OpenClaw can autonomously browse sites and detect “event‑sponsor” signals; Firecrawl / PhantomBuster structure public data for ingestion.
    

## Enrichment

- **Best**: **Clay** + **Apollo**.
    
    - Clay: enriches companies with tech stack, social signals, and event‑related activity.
        
    - Apollo: provides B2B contact data (email, role) for sales outreach.
        

## Outreach

- **Best**: **Instantly / Lemlist** (email) + **WhatsApp API** (e.g., via 360dialog / Twilio) + **OpenClaw** as orchestrator.
    
    - Instantly / Lemlist: high‑volume, warm‑up‑aware email campaigns.
        
    - WhatsApp: primary for Medellín; use verified business accounts for compliant broadcasts.
        

## Automation

- **Best**: **OpenClaw** (core orchestration) + **Zapier / Make** (connector glue) + in‑house API (for mdeai data).
    
    - OpenClaw handles multi‑step workflows and “agent‑like” autonomy.
        
    - Zapier / Make link CRM, email, and analytics tools.
        

## Analytics

- **Best**: **BigQuery / Snowflake** (data warehouse) + **Looker Studio** (sponsor dashboards) + **custom AI‑insight layer** (cust.LLM or TwelveLabs‑style multimodal analysis on event video).
    
    - BigQuery: ingest events, scans, app events, and UTM‑tagged traffic.
        
    - Looker Studio: real‑time dashboards for sponsors.
        
    - AI‑insight layer: explains why activations perform a certain way.

## 7. Step‑by‑step implementation plan

## Phase 1: Foundation (0–4 weeks)

1. Map sponsor personas and historical data (what worked in Medellín nightlife / fashion).
    
2. Build a basic sponsor dashboard in mdeai (event + sponsor + KPIs).
    
3. Integrate UTM tracking and QR‑link logging.
    
4. Set up BigQuery / Snowflake pipeline for event data.
    

## Phase 2: AI & tools (4–8 weeks)

1. Deploy OpenClaw locally and configure:
    
    - Discovery skill (Google Ads, Instagram, LinkedIn).
        
    - Outreach skill (Instantly / WhatsApp API).
        
2. Connect Clay + Apollo for enrichment.
    
3. Train AI‑content generator on:
    
    - Brand decks.
        
    - Past high‑performing creatives.
        
4. Set up Looker Studio dashboards for sponsors.
    

## Phase 3: Activation pilots (8–12 weeks)

1. Launch 3–5 core activations:
    
    - WhatsApp early‑bird + QR “Scan & Win”.
        
    - TikTok challenge.
        
    - Sponsored VIP carousel.
        
2. A/B test 2 creatives per sponsor per channel.
    
3. Gather feedback and refine AI‑insight messages.
    

## Phase 4: Scale & innovate (12–20 weeks)

1. Roll out:
    
    - Dynamic pricing slots.
        
    - Performance‑based pricing packages.
        
    - AI‑generated highlight reels.
        
2. Extend into Colombiamoda / Feria de Flores‑aligned packages.
    
3. Activate Deep‑Latin‑American‑fashion‑sponsors via OpenClaw‑driven outreach
