# Phase 3 — Event marketer (promotion + ROI)

> **Purpose.** Treat every event as a campaign, not a checkbox. Promotion → registration → attendance → engagement → follow-up → close. Each is a conversion point.

## Philosophy — four rules

1. **Event as funnel.** Promotion, attendance, engagement, and follow-up are all conversion points with measurable rates.
2. **Quality over quantity.** 50 qualified conversations beat 500 badge scans.
3. **Experience creates memory.** What attendees remember matters more than what you said.
4. **The event ends when the deal closes.** Post-event execution is where ROI lives.

## The event marketing funnel

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   PROMOTE  →  REGISTER  →  ATTEND  →  ENGAGE  →  FOLLOW UP │
│   (reach)    (convert)    (show)     (capture)  (nurture)  │
│                                                              │
│                          ↓                                   │
│                       CLOSE                                  │
│                       (win)                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Goal framework

| Goal | Primary metric | Secondary metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Brand awareness | Impressions, booth traffic | Social mentions, press coverage |
| Lead generation | MQLs generated | Cost per lead, lead quality score |
| Pipeline acceleration | Meetings booked | Opportunities influenced |
| Customer retention | NPS lift, engagement | Renewal mentions, expansion convos |
| Thought leadership | Speaking slots, content downloads | Media mentions, social engagement |
| Partnership development | Partner meetings | Joint opportunities identified |

Pick **one** primary goal per event. Multiple goals = no goal.

## EPIC checklist

The four-phase execution checklist. Each phase has its own tasks; skipping any one undermines the next.

### E — Execute pre-event

- ☐ Promotion timeline + channels (see "promotion stack" below)
- ☐ Registration page optimization (one CTA, one form, one path)
- ☐ Pre-event nurture sequence (3 emails minimum: announce / agenda / day-before)
- ☐ Booth / materials preparation (collateral, swag, equipment, shipping)
- ☐ Speaker prep (talking points, slide review, AV needs)
- ☐ Pre-scheduled meetings (target ICP companies, calendar invites locked 2 weeks out)

### P — Perform at event

- ☐ Staff training + talking points (everyone speaks the brand voice)
- ☐ Lead capture system (single source of truth, integrated with CRM)
- ☐ Engagement activities (demos, contests, photo ops)
- ☐ Real-time content capture (photos, quotes, social moments)
- ☐ Booth schedule (rotations, breaks, key time-blocks for VIPs)

### I — Implement follow-up

- ☐ Lead scoring + routing (per ICP fit + engagement level)
- ☐ Personalized follow-up sequences (T+1d hot leads, T+3d warm, T+7d cold)
- ☐ Content delivery (recordings, slides, photos within 7 days)
- ☐ Meeting booking (calendar links in every follow-up)
- ☐ Sales handoff (what does sales see vs marketing keeps)

### C — Calculate ROI

- ☐ Lead attribution (UTM, salesforce campaign, manual tagging)
- ☐ Pipeline tracking (which opportunities trace to this event)
- ☐ Revenue attribution (closed deals at T+30d / T+60d / T+90d / T+180d)
- ☐ Learnings documentation (what worked, what didn't, decisions for next time)
- ☐ Sponsor reporting (if you sponsored, deliver the recap PDF)

## Event metrics by stage

| Stage | Metrics | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Promotion | Email open rate, CTR, social engagement | 25%+ open, 3%+ CTR |
| Registration | Registration rate, cost per registration | 2–5% of audience |
| Attendance | Show rate, check-in time | 40–60% webinar, 80%+ in-person paid |
| Engagement | Booth visits, session attendance, Q&A | 50%+ session completion |
| Follow-up | Response rate, meetings booked | 15%+ response, 5%+ meetings |
| Conversion | MQL → SQL rate, pipeline generated | 20%+ MQL → SQL |

## Promotion timeline (full rollout)

| Phase | Timing | Channels | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Announcement | 6–9 months out | Website, email, social | Save-the-date, early bird |
| Early bird | 4–6 months out | Email, ads, partnerships | Pricing incentive, speakers |
| Main campaign | 2–4 months out | All channels, PR | Full agenda, testimonials |
| Urgency push | 2–4 weeks out | Email, retargeting, social | Limited spots, final speakers |

## Anti-patterns

- **Spray-and-pray sponsorships** — sponsoring every event without ICP alignment.
- **Badge-scanning obsession** — quantity of leads over quality of conversations.
- **No pre-event outreach** — showing up cold without scheduled meetings.
- **Post-event black hole** — leads die in a spreadsheet instead of sequences.
- **Same booth everywhere** — not adapting presence to event audience.
- **Measuring attendance, not pipeline** — vanity metrics instead of business impact.
- **Speaker with no follow-up** — great talk, no content capture or attendee nurture.
- **One-and-done events** — missing the compound effect of consistent presence.
- **Paid voting before guaranteed value** — early-stage platforms can't credibly claim CPL until 30+ days of data exist.
