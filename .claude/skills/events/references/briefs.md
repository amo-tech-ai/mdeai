# Phase 1 — Event briefs

> **Purpose.** Align stakeholders on goal, audience, tier, narrative, and measurement BEFORE production starts. The brief is the contract everyone signs against. If the brief is fuzzy, every downstream decision will be fuzzy.

## When to write a brief

- Kicking off ANY event (webinar, field, flagship, internal, external)
- Aligning cross-functional partners before vendors are booked
- Handing event concepts to producers, regional teams, or contract help
- Recurring series — write the brief once, version it per iteration

## The 6-section framework

Every brief has these six sections in this order. None can be skipped without a documented reason.

### 1. Overview

- **Goal** — one sentence in observable terms. "Generate 50 MQLs from Brazilian fintech" beats "raise awareness."
- **Target audience** — primary persona, secondary persona, and the explicit "not us" set.
- **Date(s)** — including buffer dates for setup/teardown.
- **Format** — in-person / virtual / hybrid; sit-down / standing / cocktail.
- **Tier** — Tier 1 (flagship, $100k+, 6+ months prep) / Tier 2 ($20k–$100k, 2–3 months) / Tier 3 (under $20k, weeks).

### 2. Narrative

- **Theme** — the one phrase that anchors all creative. "The future of work in LatAm" beats "tech event."
- **Key messages** — 3 max. If you have 5, you have none.
- **CTA** — what attendees should do at the end. "Book a demo at booth 42" beats "engage with us."
- **Proof assets** — testimonials, case studies, data points that back the messages.

### 3. Experience

- **Agenda** — block-level (Welcome / Keynote / Breakout 1 / Lunch / Panel / Workshop / Close).
- **Speakers** — confirmed + tentative + asks. With each speaker: their session topic + 50-word bio + AV needs.
- **Demos / activations** — what people interact with in the room (not on stage).
- **Special moments** — opening, midpoint energy boost, finale.

### 4. Promotion

- **Channels** — owned (email, website), earned (PR, press), paid (ads, retargeting), partner (co-promo).
- **Audience segments** — for each channel, who's targeted.
- **Partner amplification** — speakers' audiences, sponsor audiences, community partner reach.
- **Timeline** — see `marketer.md` for the full 6–9 month rollout pattern.

### 5. Operations

- **Budget** — gross + per-line + 10% contingency. See `planner.md` for the % split by category.
- **Vendors** — venue, catering, AV, photography, security, transport, rentals, printing — each with status (researching / proposed / contracted).
- **Timeline** — milestone dates from 12 months out to day-of.
- **Risks** — top 5 with mitigation owner.
- **Approvals needed** — legal, brand, finance, executive sponsor — with target dates.

### 6. Measurement

- **KPIs** — leading (registration rate, RSVP rate) + lagging (MQLs, pipeline, NPS).
- **Dashboards** — where the numbers live (Salesforce / mixpanel / Notion / spreadsheet).
- **Survey plan** — pre-event (audience research), in-event (live polling), post-event (NPS + qualitative).
- **Follow-up flow** — what happens to a lead at T+1d / T+7d / T+30d.

## Reviewer checklist

Before signing off:

- ✅ Approvers + owners defined for every section.
- ✅ Dependencies captured (product readiness, content, sales enablement, legal, brand).
- ✅ Data instrumentation planned (UTMs, Salesforce campaign IDs, GA events).
- ✅ Post-event follow-up mapped (who, when, what content).
- ✅ "Not us" exclusions written down (prevents scope creep).
- ✅ Top 3 risks have named mitigation owners.

## Tips

- **Keep briefs in shared workspace, version per iteration.** Don't email a PDF; that version dies.
- **Reuse sections for series; highlight delta vs prior runs** — most of the brief stays stable; the delta is what matters.
- **Pair with vendor tracker + day-of run sheet** outputs (see `planner.md`) — the brief drives those documents.

## Anti-patterns

- **Skipping the "Not us" section** — leads to scope creep and audience confusion.
- **Vague theme** ("future of innovation") — every breakout looks the same.
- **5+ key messages** — attendees remember none.
- **Budget without contingency** — first vendor surprise sinks the whole event.
- **Measurement after the fact** — instrumentation needs to exist BEFORE the event opens.
