# Phase 4 — Hosting (Luma tactics for small founder events)

> **Purpose.** Tactical hosting playbook for small (≤200 attendees) founder-led events on Luma or similar platforms. For larger events, escalate to `planner.md` + `marketer.md`.

## When to use this layer

- Founder hosting a meetup, demo night, workshop, fireside chat
- Recurring community series (monthly AI founders meetup, weekly DJ contest, etc.)
- Lead-gen / pipeline events (≤30 attendees, very curated)
- First-time event organizers learning the basics

## Format selection

| Format | Best for | Typical size | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fireside chat | Thought leadership + intimate discussion | 20–50 | Low |
| Panel | Diverse perspectives + networking draw | 30–100 | Medium |
| Workshop | Teaching + product education + lead gen | 15–30 | Medium |
| Demo night | Showcasing products + community | 30–80 | Medium |
| Hackathon | Developer community + product feedback | 20–100 | High |
| Dinner / small gathering | Executive networking | 8–15 | Low (high cost) |

## Concept — write it down before anything else

- **Goal:** what does success look like? (X attendees, Y leads, Z brand impressions, all observable)
- **Format:** panel / workshop / demo night / fireside / networking / hackathon
- **Audience:** who specifically should attend, and why they'd come
- **Value prop for attendees:** what they'll learn, who they'll meet, what they'll get
- **Date / time:** check holiday + competitor + venue conflicts
- **Capacity:** seated vs standing; intimate vs broad
- **Platform:** Luma URL or venue address

## Luma event page — what makes it convert

### Title

Specific and compelling. Avoid "Tech Meetup #4". Try:
- ✅ "How 5 YC Founders Got Their First 100 Customers"
- ✅ "Demo Night: 6 Latin AI Startups in 60 Minutes"
- ✅ "Workshop: Build Your First Supabase RLS Policy in 90 Minutes"
- ❌ "AI Meetup"
- ❌ "Tech Talk"

### Description (150–300 words)

Structure:

1. **Hook** — start with the problem the attendee has, or the result they want.
2. **What you'll cover** — 3 bullet points of takeaway value.
3. **Who should attend** — be explicit (and exclude the wrong people gracefully).
4. **Speaker bios** — 30 words each, with specific credibility marker (not "expert in X" — say "founder of [company], previously [credential]").
5. **Logistics** — date/time/location/parking/accessibility.

### Image / banner

- Luma dimensions: 1600×900 px
- High-contrast, readable thumbnail at 200px wide
- Speaker face if it's a one-person event
- Brand colors but not a logo dump

## Run-of-show — minute-by-minute agenda

```
TIME      | SEGMENT                  | OWNER     | NOTES
6:00 PM   | Doors open               | —         | Networking, food/drinks (15-min buffer)
6:15 PM   | Welcome                  | Founder   | Context, housekeeping (5 min — who you are, why this event exists)
6:20 PM   | Main content block       | Speaker(s)| 30–45 min — talks, panels, demos
7:05 PM   | Q&A / discussion         | Moderator | 15 min
7:20 PM   | Networking / unstructured| —         | 30 min for in-person
7:50 PM   | Close + next steps       | Founder   | Call to action: join community, try product, attend next
8:00 PM   | Doors close              | —         |
```

## Promotion plan — fill the seats

| Channel | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Luma | Publish event page | 3 weeks before |
| LinkedIn | Founder post + speaker reshares | 2 weeks before |
| Communities | Post in [specific Slack / Discord / forum] | 2 weeks before |
| Direct outreach | Personal invite to 20–30 anchor attendees | 2 weeks before |
| Reminder | Luma auto-reminder + personal note | 1 day before |

**The anchor attendee strategy** — invite 20–30 people personally who make the event worth attending for everyone else. This is the highest-leverage move in small-event hosting.

## Follow-up plan

- ☐ Same-day thank-you email with key takeaways (within 6 hours)
- ☐ Share recording / slides within 48 hours (if applicable)
- ☐ Personal follow-up with 5–10 high-value attendees (T+3d)
- ☐ Announce next event date (if recurring)

## Luma-specific tips

- **"Require Approval"** to curate attendees for quality > quantity
- **"Ask a Question"** during registration to qualify attendees
- **Co-hosts** so speakers can also invite their networks
- **Calendar subscription** for recurring events (one-tap follow)
- **Built-in reminders have high open rates** — don't duplicate with external email

## The 40% rule

- **Free events:** expect 40–60% of RSVPs to actually attend. Overbook accordingly (RSVP target = capacity × 1.7).
- **Paid events ($25+):** expect 80–90% attendance.
- **Free dinners with curated guest list:** 90%+ if personal invites.

## Making events a growth channel

- **Host monthly, not "whenever".** Consistency builds an audience that returns.
- **Each event has a natural next step** — join community, try product, attend the next event.
- **Record everything.** One event becomes 5–10 pieces of content (clips, quotes, blog posts, social posts).
- **Best events are conversations, not presentations.** Leave 30%+ of time unstructured.

## Common mistakes

- ❌ Making it about your product instead of your audience's interests.
- ❌ Inviting everyone instead of curating for quality.
- ❌ No follow-up after the event (the event is the beginning, not the end).
- ❌ Over-programming — leave room for serendipity and networking.
- ❌ Not starting until everything is "perfect" — first event can be 15 people in a coffee shop.
- ❌ No clear next step — attendees walk away with nothing to do.

## Examples

### Prompt: monthly AI founders meetup in SF (50 attendees)

- Format: panel + networking
- Title: "How 5 YC Founders Got Their First 100 Customers"
- 3-week promo cycle, anchor invite 20 founders
- Sponsor (food + drinks) covers $500 budget
- Recap = 5-tweet thread + 1 LinkedIn post + 1 short YouTube video

### Prompt: virtual workshop teaching API usage to developers

- Format: workshop (15–30 attendees)
- 90 min: 30 min teach + 45 min live coding + 15 min Q&A
- Promo through dev communities + speaker's audience
- Lead capture: API key signup with workshop coupon
- Follow-up: tutorial blog post + Discord invite

### Prompt: weekly DJ contest at Laureles bar (Phase 1 mdeai micro-contest)

- Format: micro-contest (4 contestants, 1 night)
- Single Bronze sponsor ($500 budget)
- Free RSVP via Luma; bonus votes per RSVP
- Promo: Luma + bar's IG Story + DJs' personal audiences
- Follow-up: winner social post + announce next week's DJs
